# Voice

## North-star phrase

> *Aj krátky čas venovaný sebe je viac ako nič.*

This is the brand promise. It gives permission. It assumes the reader is tired. Protect it.

## Principles

1. **Speak to a friend, not an audience.** Second person singular ("ty"), direct, warm.
2. **Be specific, not aspirational.** "15 minút denne" beats "just a few minutes a day." Numbers over adjectives.
3. **Give permission, not instruction.** "Môžeš začať aj dnes" beats "Start today!"
4. **Quiet confidence, not exclamation.** No `!` in long-form copy. Calm voice = confident voice.
5. **Embodiment, not performance.** "Cítiť sa ako ja" beats "transform your body."
6. **Acknowledge reality.** She's tired. She doesn't have time. The copy knows this before she says it.

## Rules

### Do

- Use second person singular ("ty", "tvoj", "tvoja")
- Use specific numbers (15 minút, 4 týždne, 7 dní)
- Use present tense by default
- Use short sentences for emphasis, long sentences for warmth
- Quote Gabi directly where possible

### Don't

- ❌ Exclamation marks in body copy
- ❌ "Achieve", "transform", "unlock", "journey" (wellness-industrial clichés)
- ❌ Emoji
- ❌ Capitalised labels translated from English ("SIGN UP" → "REGISTRÁCIA")
- ❌ Formal "vy" form — NeoMe is not a bank
- ❌ Translations from English marketing copy

## For Claude Code specifically

**Never invent Slovak strings.** If a feature needs new microcopy:

1. Draft in English in a PR comment
2. Ask the user (or Gabi) to translate or approve
3. Only commit Slovak strings after approval

Translated-from-English wellness copy is instantly detectable to a Slovak reader and damages trust. This is the single easiest way to accidentally break the brand.

## Eyebrow copywriting

Eyebrows are 1–3 words, UPPERCASE (via CSS, not in source), Slovak. They label the section, not the content.

Good: `SILA` · `POKOJ` · `KOMUNITA` · `TVOJ CYKLUS` · `PROGRAMY`
Bad: `🌿 POWERED BY SCIENCE` · `THE WELLNESS SECTION` · `CLICK HERE TO CONTINUE!`

## Button copywriting

Buttons are verbs in 1–3 words. Slovak imperative, second person singular where a verb needs a subject.

Good: `Začni teraz` · `Pokračuj` · `Prihlás sa` · `Vyskúšaj zadarmo` · `Čítaj viac`
Bad: `CLICK HERE` · `SUBMIT` · `LEARN MORE!!!` · `Začnite` (too formal)
